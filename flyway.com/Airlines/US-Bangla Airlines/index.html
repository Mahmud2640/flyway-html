US-Bangla Airlines Baggage Allowance

    Carry-on Baggage
    Flying passengers to US-Bangla Airlines are allowed to carry one item per customer. The luggage should be no more massive than the allowance weighing 7 kg and should not exceed the dimensions of 15 x 22 x 3 cm (6 x 8 x 14 inches). This applies to adults, children, and infants on both international and domestic flights.

    Checked Baggage
    On international flights, US-Bangla Airlines passengers are allowed one item of checked baggage as a customer. Checked luggage for economy class passengers must not weigh more than 20kg, and business class passengers have an allowance of 30kg. This applies to both adults and children. Infant’s luggage must not weigh more than 10kg for both business and economy classes. All luggage does not have to exceed 40 x 60 x 100 cm (15x24x40 in).

    On domestic flights, adults and children are allowed 20 kg of checked baggage for all classes. Infants are allowed 10 kilograms of testing baggage. All tested bags do not have to exceed the dimensions of 40 x 60 x 100 cm (15x24x40 in).

 

About US-Bangla Airlines

    US-Bangla Airlines are a Bangladeshi airline based in the Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (DAC), Dhaka. The airline was launched on 17 July 2014, initially with only two domestic cities within Bangladesh – Shah Amanat International Airport (CGP), Chittagong, and Jessore Airport (JSR). Before the end of the year, US-Bangla Airlines doubled its flight network and started international flights to Cox’s Bazar Airport (CXB) and Saidpur Airport (SPD).

    July 2016 – Two years after the airline’s inauguration, US-Bangla Airlines launches its first service to international destinations such as Singapore and Dubai. The airline now flies to 14 different goals from Dhaka’s center, consisting of seven domestic cities and seven global cities. The Bangladeshi carrier fleet currently has 4 Boeing 737-800s and 3 Bombardier Q400s, each with three more orders.

    On April 29, 2019, the airline started flights to Guangzhou, becoming the first Bangladeshi airline to operate flights to China. The airline has become the first Bangladeshi carrier to operate flights to Chennai on 31 March 2019 in South India.
